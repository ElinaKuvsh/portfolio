VitaCrop→

Project Background

This project challenges the myth that aeroponics and hydroponics are only for large, expensive farms. It’s designed to promote equipment sales to small businesses, restaurants, caterers, and even households. The target audience—rational thinkers who value quality, sustainability, and efficiency—will encounter the brand through the website, app, outdoor ads, and point-of-sale materials.

The proposed solution uses rich browns, vibrant greens, and a hint of orange to symbolize stability, growth, and innovation. The logo—a stylized plant in a geometric container within a circle—represents nurture and sustainability. Rounded forms create a friendly, natural feel, balanced by angular leaf patterns that add rhythm and structure.

←ZapNoods

Project Background

A small instant noodle company wants distinctive packaging for its first and only flavor, aimed at students and teenagers. Since this is their first contact with consumers, the focus is on creating a unique taste and matching packaging to stand out.

I chose a pink and purple palette to break away from typical red-food associations. Pastel tones appeal to students who like the "study aesthetic," while a space_invader emoji hints at a younger vibe. The design also highlights the noodles’ nutritional value and gluten-free formula as key advantages.

WatchList

Project Background

This mobile-first app was designed for a small group of friends to track movies they want to watch and rate the ones they’ve seen. The design was tailored to Google Pixel 7 screen sizes. As my first UX case study, I focused on learning core Figma skills: building reusable components, managing their versions, and maintaining a consistent color palette. With no strict visual constraints beyond functionality, I chose a clean, minimalist look to simplify front-end implementation. Fonts used were Koh Santepheap and Kdam Thmor Pro (mostly Regular), with a neutral palette of dusty green, light green, and pearl.

I chose a pink and purple palette to break away from typical red-food associations. Pastel tones appeal to students who like the "study aesthetic," while a space_invader emoji hints at a younger vibe.
